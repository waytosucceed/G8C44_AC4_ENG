Writing Task: Mastering the Art of Personification

Instructions: Write a brief narrative or description (4-6 sentences) for each prompt, clearly illustrating the specific form of personification. Ensure your writing is coherent, cohesive, and effectively conveys the intended emotion or message.


Prompts:

  • Emotional Personification: Describe how a feeling (e.g., happiness, sadness, or fear) can take on human-like qualities. For example, how does sadness act when it visits someone?
  • Anthropomorphic Personification: Write about an animal or object behaving like a human. What does a wise old tree say to a child who seeks shelter under its branches?
  • Allegorical Personification: Create a character based on an abstract idea (e.g., justice, love, or curiosity) and depict their role in a moral lesson. How does Curiosity encourage someone to explore beyond their comfort zone?
  • Extended Personification: Focus on a single object or element in nature and develop its human-like qualities over a few sentences. How does the moon interact with the stars and the night sky?
